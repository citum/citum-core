Locales and Gender-Aware Terms

Use locale terms for roles, labels, locators, and grammatical agreement.

Locale-owned text

Use locale terms for text that varies by language: page labels, role labels, and other bibliographic terms. Style templates should express structure; locale files should own language.

- term: volume
  form: short
  suffix: " "
- number: volume

Contributor gender metadata

Contributor-driven role labels can use contributor gender metadata where the locale provides gendered forms.

contributors:
  - role: editor
    contributor:
      family: "Martinez"
      given: "Ana"
    gender: feminine

Mixed-gender contributor groups prefer a locale's neutral or common form when one exists. Citum does not silently choose a masculine-specific label for a mixed group when only gendered forms are available.

Template gender overrides

Use a template-level gender override when the style needs to request a specific agreement form directly.

- contributor: editor
  form: long
  gender: feminine

- term: volume
  form: short
  gender: masculine

- number: volume
  label-form: short
  gender: feminine

Locale YAML

Locale terms can be plain strings or gendered maps.

roles:
  editor:
    long:
      singular:
        masculine: editor
        feminine: editora
        common: persona editora
      plural:
        masculine: editores
        feminine: editoras
        common: equipo editorial

Locator terms can also declare lexical gender metadata.

locators:
  page:
    long:
      singular: página
      plural: páginas
    short:
      singular: p.
      plural: pp.
    gender: feminine
ℹ️
Current scope Gender-aware rendering applies to locale term selection and contributor role labels. Verb-form role terms remain ungendered until the follow-up adds the required plumbing.
