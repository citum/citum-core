Integral and non-integral citations

Every Citum style declares separate templates for integral (narrative) and non-integral (parenthetical) citation forms. The same input data renders differently depending on whether the author name is grammatically part of the sentence.

This page shows the same two-citation passage rendered by four classes of citation style: author-date, numeric and note-based (combined, since the integral/non-integral logic is identical), and label.

Illustration only. The passage text and references below are entirely artificial. See the full demo for a richer example with multilingual sources, EDTF dates, and archival references.

Input

The same two references are used across all style-class sections below.

Key Author(s) Year Title Type
chen2017 Chen, M. 2017 The Social Life of References book
okafor2024 Okafor, N.; Ferreira, B.; Park, J. 2024 Network Topology of Academic Citation report (preprint)

Full YAML definition: docs/demo-refs.yaml

Author-date

Representative style: APA 7th. The in-text citation carries the author surname and year. Integral form folds the author name into the sentence; non-integral places author and year in parentheses.

Integral (narrative)
The mechanisms by which academic knowledge is attributed have attracted renewed scholarly attention. Chen (2017) has argued that citation practices encode social hierarchies as much as intellectual ones. More recently, Okafor et al. (2024) provided computational evidence for this view at scale.
Non-integral (parenthetical)
The mechanisms by which academic knowledge is attributed have attracted renewed scholarly attention. Citation practices encode social hierarchies as much as intellectual ones (Chen, 2017). Computational evidence for this view has recently been provided at scale (Okafor et al., 2024).
Rendered citation forms — APA 7th
Integral             Chen (2017)          Okafor et al. (2024)
Integral + locator   Chen (2017, p. 42)   Okafor et al. (2024, p. 8)
Non-integral         (Chen, 2017)         (Okafor et al., 2024)
Non-integral + loc   (Chen, 2017, p. 42)  (Okafor et al., 2024, p. 8)

Numeric and note-based

These two classes share the same integral/non-integral logic: the citation marker (a number or a note reference) can appear either adjacent to the author name (integral) or anchored to the clause end (non-integral). The difference is purely in marker form: a bracketed integer [1] for numeric styles, a superscript note reference 1 for note-based styles.

Representative styles: IEEE (numeric), Chicago Notes (note-based).

Integral — numeric (IEEE)
The mechanisms by which academic knowledge is attributed have attracted renewed scholarly attention. Chen [1] has argued that citation practices encode social hierarchies as much as intellectual ones. More recently, Okafor et al. [2] provided computational evidence for this view at scale.
Non-integral — numeric (IEEE)
The mechanisms by which academic knowledge is attributed have attracted renewed scholarly attention. Citation practices encode social hierarchies as much as intellectual ones [1]. Computational evidence for this view has recently been provided at scale [2].
Integral — note-based (Chicago)
The mechanisms by which academic knowledge is attributed have attracted renewed scholarly attention. Chen1 has argued that citation practices encode social hierarchies as much as intellectual ones. More recently, Okafor et al.2 provided computational evidence for this view at scale.
1 Chen Mei, The Social Life of References (MIT Press, 2017), p. 12.
2 Nnamdi Okafor, Beatriz Ferreira, and Jiyeon Park, “Network Topology of Academic Citation,” arXiv:2401.05832 (2024).
Non-integral — note-based (Chicago)
The mechanisms by which academic knowledge is attributed have attracted renewed scholarly attention. Citation practices encode social hierarchies as much as intellectual ones.1 Computational evidence for this view has recently been provided at scale.2
1 Chen, Social Life of References, 12.
2 Okafor, Ferreira, and Park, “Network Topology,” 2401.05832.
Name memory in note styles
First note:     Chen Mei, The Social Life of References (MIT Press, 2017), p. 12.
Later note:     Chen, Social Life of References, 42.   ← abbreviated (name memory)

Label-based

Representative style: alphanumeric (e.g., ACM, alpha). The citation marker is a short mnemonic derived from author surnames and year rather than an assigned integer. Integral and non-integral placement follow the same logic as other classes: either adjacent to the author name or at the clause end.

Integral
The mechanisms by which academic knowledge is attributed have attracted renewed scholarly attention. Chen [Che17] has argued that citation practices encode social hierarchies as much as intellectual ones. More recently, Okafor et al. [OFP24] provided computational evidence for this view at scale.
Non-integral
The mechanisms by which academic knowledge is attributed have attracted renewed scholarly attention. Citation practices encode social hierarchies as much as intellectual ones [Che17]. Computational evidence for this view has recently been provided at scale [OFP24].
Rendered citation forms — label (alpha)
Integral       Chen [Che17]          Okafor et al. [OFP24]
Non-integral   [Che17]               [OFP24]
